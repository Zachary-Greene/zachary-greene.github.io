PCB Christmas Tree

This project is a christmas tree that is made up of two PCBs that are soldered together using exposed copper pads on the edge that, when soldered, will create a free standing christmas decoration. The needed components for the project are: one ATtiny85 SOP Package from Digikey (ATTINY85-20SURCT-ND), a Cr2032 Coin Cell Holder from Core Electronics or Sparkfun (PRT-00783), a CR2032 Coin Cell, eleven APA102C-5050 RGB LED's from Core Electronics or Sparkfun (COM-16345), and one 0603 1KÎ© Resistor from Digikey (311-1KMCT-ND). I got all my parts from either Digikey or Core Electronics, but you can find the same parts from places like Sparkfun, Adafruit or Element14.

To assemble the PCB's, solder the smallest components on the boards first otherwise it'll be harder if you solder the bigger parts beforehand. Then solder the larger components such as the battery holder. Afterwards, solder the two boards together with a strong connection, to prevent the solder from dripping down the board, slot them together and put them on their sides so that they form a 'V' shape then solder.
